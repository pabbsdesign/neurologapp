# The Why: From Roadblocks to Research

NeuroLog was born out of the clinical gaps encountered during the treatment journey for mechanical neurological symptoms. This project represents a shift from passive tracking to active investigation—turning data points into actionable clinical insights.

### The Problem
Traditional symptom trackers are often "flat"—they record *that* a symptom happened, but rarely *why*. For patients with conditions like IIH or Chiari, the standard medical advice to "track your triggers" becomes impossible when those triggers are constant, mechanical, or positional. This tool addresses three major roadblocks:

1. **Trigger Complexity:** Standard apps do not account for Valsalva maneuvers, positional shifts, or the weighted impact of physical exertion.
2. **The Data-Emotion Loop:** Without objective scaling, it is easy to lose sight of actual functional progress during high-pain days. 
3. **Clinical Communication:** It is difficult to explain the "Resilience Gap"—the difference between physical symptom severity and one's ability to mentally navigate that pain—to clinicians without hard data.

### The Solution
I created this app to move from passive logging to **active investigation**. By building a weighted attribution model, NeuroLog attempts to filter the "noise" of daily life, helping to isolate which mechanical movements are the true drivers of symptoms. 

This is a tool built to turn the complexity of a neurological diagnosis into a structured, patient-led research methodology.
